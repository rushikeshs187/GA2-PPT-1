Palestine's 10-Year Economic Strategy

(2026-2036): A Vision for Sovereignty and Resilience

The New Vision: Sumood as an Economic Doctrine

From Dependency to Sovereignty

This 10-year strategy is not a "post-oil" vision. It is a strategic pivot toward a "post-aid" and "post-conflict" economy defined by resilience, self-sufficiency, and Economic Sovereignty.

What is Sumood (Steadfastness)?

A "resistance and resilience economy" designed to mitigate the primary constraints imposed upon it. This strategy translates that cultural imperative into an actionable model.

Land

Restrictions, especially in Area C (60% of West Bank), limit physical expansion.

Water

Constraints on water access necessitate high-efficiency solutions like agritech.

Movement

Checkpoints & barriers constrain the movement of goods and people.

Pillar 1: The Digital Economy ("Silicon Palestine")

Human Capital as a Natural Resource

This pillar leverages Palestine's greatest asset: its educated, ambitious, and youthful population.

  • Strategic Shift: Move from "Brain Drain" to "Brain Gain" by fostering a world-class remote workforce.
  • Action 1: Rebuild and support iconic tech hubs and accelerators like Gaza Sky Geeks.
  • Action 2: Launch "PalestiNext" global hubs in cities like London and Dubai to connect diaspora capital with local entrepreneurs.

Pillars 2 & 3: A Resilient Foundation

Energy Sovereignty

The Goal: Break the 86% dependency on imported electricity, a critical economic and strategic vulnerability.

Action: Scale "Noor Palestine" (solar schools) & the "Taqa" joint venture.

Food Sovereignty (Agritech)

The Goal: Directly combat famine (IPC Phase 5) and build a resilient food supply.

The Action: Mass-deploy high-tech solutions like hydroponics, decoupling food production from land and water constraints.

90% Less Water

Used by hydroponic systems compared to traditional agriculture.

Pillar 4 (Tourism) & The Talent Engine

Solving the "Talent Paradox"

Palestine has high graduation rates but a critical "skills mismatch." The 10-year strategy is to align education with market needs.

43.5%

Graduate unemployment (youth with diplomas) due to this skills gap.

  • Higher Ed Alignment: Implement the 2025 Al-Quds Labor Market Survey to refine curricula.
  • Vocational Excellence (TVET): Scale up high-quality technical training with proven job placement.

Investment & Funding Models

Sovereign Leadership

The Palestine Investment Fund (PIF) will lead and de-risk large-scale national projects (e.g., "Massader" for energy).

Re-booting SMEs

€400M

A new EIB facility to inject vital liquidity and risk-sharing to stabilize banks and fund SMEs.

Diaspora Capital

7 Million+

Formalize investment from the strong global diaspora, moving beyond remittance to fund entrepreneurs.

Blended Finance

Use donor grants to "buy down" risk for private investors in critical sectors like green energy and tech.

Strategic Timeline (Phased Rollout)

Years 1-3 (2026-2028)

Recovery & Foundation

  • Deploy €400M EIB facility to stabilize SMEs
  • Begin curriculum reform based on 2025 survey
  • Rebuild digital hubs (e.g., Gaza Sky Geeks)
  • Launch rooftop solar & hydroponics at scale
Years 4-7 (2029-2032)

Scale & Connect

  • Launch large-scale "Taqa" energy projects
  • Open first "PalestiNext" global hubs
  • Market "Made in Palestine" brand to EU
  • First market-aligned cohort graduates
Years 8-10 (2033-2036)

Sovereignty & Integration

  • Achieve 50% domestic renewable energy
  • Create 50,000+ new high-value digital jobs
  • Reduce single-partner trade dependency
  • Establish Palestine as a brand of innovation
